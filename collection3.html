    • Raf Simons' obsession with Americana was never made so apparent as his Autumn / Winter 2002 "Virginia Creeper" collection. Named after a scenic trail in the mountains of rural Virginia, which itself was named after a flora common on said trail, the inspiration for this collection was American small-town horror films, particularly those centering on high school students and athletic cultures of their institutions. Classic varsity apparel like letterman jackets and printed sweatshirts were paired with suggestive oversized "caged" pants and bomber jackets, evoking imagery of horror and murder. Select details on many of the garments gave subtle hints to terrifying scenarios, such as patches stating "U.F.O." or sweatshirts so distressed they are nearly falling apart.
